*WELL-ENGINEERED, STURDY DOORS - THE SOBHA WAY*
A door is opened & closed thousands of times in its lifetime. At Sobha, it is engineered, not just installed.

What makes Sobha doors different?
Imported Beech Wood
Sobha doors are made using Beech wood imported from Europe - known for its strength, stability and long life. The *Honey-comb structure* makes the base of all doors and ensures doors do not warp easily and maintain their shape for years.

Precision Tolerance (3–4 mm)
Door frames are fitted with a controlled tolerance of 3–4 mm, allowing the door to “breathe” during high humidity and seasonal changes. 
Result: smooth operation in all weather conditions.

PU Foam Bonding (Nail-less Fitment)
Instead of nails, PU foam is used as the bonding agent. This provides:
Strong adhesion
Shock absorption
Zero rusting
No loosening over time

Protection for Walls
PU foam absorbs shocks when doors are shut.
No shocks means no cracks in adjacent walls — even after decades.

Silicone & Door Seals
Proper sealing prevents air gaps, sound leakage and moisture ingress, enhancing comfort and durability.

🚿 The Real Root Cause of Seepage?
It’s usually the bathroom above you.
Water seepage doesn’t begin in your living room ceiling. It begins where water is used the most - the bathroom.
At Sobha, we engineer bathrooms so you never have to deal with seepage, patchwork repairs, or ceiling stains for life.

Here’s How We Do It:
Precision Waterproofing at Corners
Corners are the most vulnerable points.
We apply a dedicated waterproofing primer to seal every junction meticulously.
Rubber Caulking Protection Strips
Even if micro-cracks develop over time, our rubber caulking strips prevent water from penetrating through edges and joints.

Double-Layer Waterproofing System
We apply two full coats of waterproofing primer across the entire bathroom floor for complete sealing. We use premium-grade MYK Schomburg waterproofing primer to ensure long-term durability and protection.

Only after this multi-layer protection system is completed do we proceed with cement layering and tiling.

Message 25 – Structurally Stronger Buildings @ Day 25
Why Sobha buildings are structurally stronger
When most people look at a building, they notice the design, the height, or the amenities. But the real strength of a building lies in its structural system.
At Sobha, we use a Reinforced Cement Concrete (RCC) Shear Wall Structure, which is significantly stronger and more stable than the traditional column-beam construction. 
Typical Column-Beam Structure

In a conventional building:
* The load of the building is transferred through columns and beams
* Walls are usually non-structural partitions
* During an earthquake or tremor, the forces concentrate at certain columns
* This can create stress points and structural movement. 

RCC Shear Wall Structure (Used by Sobha)
A shear wall system distributes loads across the entire structure.
Instead of depending only on columns, large reinforced concrete walls act as the backbone of the building.

This means:
* Earthquake forces are distributed evenly across the structure. 
* The building becomes far more resistant to lateral movement. 
* Tremor loads are shared across multiple walls instead of concentrating at a few columns. 
* The structure behaves like a single solid unit. 
* ⁠Cracks dont develop on the walls because of minimal movement. 
This is why shear wall structures are widely used in high-rise buildings across the world.

Precision Construction with Aluminium Formwork
Another major advantage in Sobha construction is the use of Aluminium Formwork Systems. Most developers still rely on iron or steel formwork because of cost effectiveness, which can create minor surface inconsistencies and even rusting. 

Sobha cuta no corners and uses uses high-precision aluminium formwork, which offers:
* Monolithic casting of walls and slabs
* Higher structural accuracy
* Smooth concrete finishes
* Greater long-term durability

This method allows the entire structure to be cast as a single integrated framework, enhancing both strength and precision.
